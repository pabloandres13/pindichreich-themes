# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Busy home cooks, mostly on mobile, looking for reliable everyday recipes. Mediterranean-leaning weeknight cooking audience. They browse on mobile, follow a cook they trust, and want recipes that work the first time.

## Product Purpose

A food and recipe blog built as a WordPress Astra child theme. Surfaces the author's personal recipe collection in a warm, editorial, photography-first format. Success = a site owner who can manage content entirely through the Gutenberg editor and Customizer, and readers who can find and trust the recipes quickly.

## Brand Personality

Warm, knowledgeable, personal. Like a well-edited food-magazine column written by one person. Friendly, never fussy. Three words: warm, editorial, reliable.

## Anti-references

- Saturated SaaS cream/sand aesthetic (cream bg + thin sans + icon grid)
- Clickbait food blog: "ULTIMATE", listicles, pop-ups everywhere
- Generic food-delivery app aesthetic (card grid, rounded tiles, flat pastel)
- Heavy decoration that hurts page speed or fights photography

## Design Principles

1. **Photography is the hero** — the design recedes and lets food photos dominate. Generous whitespace, soft rounded frames, slow hover zoom.
2. **Non-technical by design** — every editable element is surfaced through Gutenberg blocks, Customizer controls, or shortcodes. No PHP editing required.
3. **Warm but not cozy-cliché** — terracotta and sage carry the warmth; the background is the Saffron & Salt cream (`#FAF7F2`), chosen deliberately, not defaulted to.
4. **Reliable before beautiful** — recipes work, pages load fast, contrast meets WCAG AA. Craft is earned, not assumed.
5. **Voice over template** — sentence case everywhere, no emoji, no marketing buzzwords. The copy sounds like a person.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Focus rings on all interactive elements (3px terracotta-alpha ring). `prefers-reduced-motion` respected — all transitions disabled. Semantic HTML throughout. Mobile-first responsive.
